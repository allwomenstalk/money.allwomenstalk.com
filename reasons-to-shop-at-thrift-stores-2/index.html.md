# The Advantages of Shopping at Thrift Stores ...

There are loads of **reasons to shop at thrift stores**. A thrift store is a shop that sells secondhand articles and especially clothes and is often run for charitable purposes. The case for thrift store shopping is a strong one. You can score great pieces on the cheap while doing your part to help your community and decrease your own environmental impact.

Nonprofit thrift stores include Goodwill, Salvation Army, and St. Vincent de Paul.

Here are the best **reasons to shop at thrift stores**.

## 1. Variety

Nothing beats the variety, which is one of the top **reasons to shop at thrift stores**. There are different styles and sizes for everyone. There is a certain element of the “thrill of the hunt” for the shopper, as well as the ability to find unique items that no one else will have. If you are frequently discouraged by the racks upon racks of size 0 items in a department store, it may be easier to search through the racks of a secondhand store where there are many different sizes.

## 2. Make Your Own Unique Sense of Style

Thrift stores are an opportunity to develop your own unique sense of style. When everyone shops at the same trendy, brand name stores, it’s inevitable that someday you’ll meet an unexpected twin in public, wearing the exact same shirt or dress as you.

## 3. It’s Easier to Spot Quality

Since most of the merchandise you come across has already been broken in, you can see what has quality and what does not. Practical and affordable.

## 4. Brand Names

If you’re an individual who worships the tag, you’re likely to find many recycled, brand-name clothes available.

## 5. New Items Daily

Thrift stores typically restock way more often than traditional retailers. As donations are made, which happens on a daily basis at large thrift stores, the items are sorted, tagged and added to the existing stock as quickly as possible.

## 6. Sales

The influx of donations makes the turnover of inventory paramount. To keep things moving out the doors and the current inventory fresh, regular sales are practically mandatory. Most thrift stores have daily, weekly and monthly sales. Half-off sales of certain items or tag colors are offered at most thrift shops.

## 7. Make It a Girls Day out

Enlist your buddies to help you shop with a purpose at thrift stores. Each of you should know the other person’s sizes and exactly what that person wants to buy. Then, as you shop, you can keep your friend’s needs in mind as well as your own.

By taking your time and shopping for the best items, you can come home with items that look like new after a little TLC. If you’re looking to make a difference, there are a variety of ways you can use your power, vote with your dollar, and make your voice heard.
