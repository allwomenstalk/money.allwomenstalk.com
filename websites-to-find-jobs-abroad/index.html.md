# 5 Awesome Websites for People Looking to Move and Work Abroad ...

Finding a job in your own city is difficult these days, but you may want move to another country and find work abroad, if you feel your are ready for this once in a lifetime experience.

There are websites to find jobs abroad you can use without that much trouble. It does seem like a challenging task, but these websites were created for people just like you.

Agencies know that talent can come from all over the world and are open to applications from anywhere. I can’t even imagine the days before the internet existed, because it would give you a lot more challenges in this department.

Well, luckily we do have the internet and these are the best websites for people looking to move and work abroad:

## 1. WORKINGABROAD.com

The name may give it away, but at least you know they are going to provide you with exactly what you are searching for. This is one of the top websites job seekers go to when looking for work in another country. I love it because it allows you to chat with your potential employer without committing. You both can get a feel and understanding if it is a good fit or not.

## 2. GOABROAD.com

This is another site for job seekers, but it offers so much more than just job ads. You are able to look for scholarships, internships and the website also helps you find the position that suits you the best. It’s almost like having a personal agent working just for you. If you are not sure where you want to travel, there is an online adviser ready to assist. This is a great help, because by completing your application more accurately, you stand a better chance of a great match.

## 3. COOLWORKS.com

You want to go with a reputable website when trying to find work abroad. The design of this website gives you an exciting feeling about what you are about to embark on. There are a lot of listings on this website and they make it look like a dream. You can find anything from a golf instructor job to an accounting officer opportunity. Basically, there is something for everyone one here and you do not have to be stuck in an office if you are a free spirit. A great place to find a variety of jobs!

## 4. EASYEXPAT.com

When you are able to upload a dozen ads a day, it seems like a great place to start. This site is very interactive and you can become part of the forum discussions. If you are unsure of the destination you’ve chosen, there is a place to ask for some advice. When you have an international forum, you are sure to find some people living in the area of your choice. Ask about culture, job opportunities and what their opinions are. You want to be absolutely sure that you are relocating to an area that is going to teach you something and that you will be happy once you get there. It is advisable to follow them on their social media accounts to help you stay up to date with the latest updates.

## 5. TRANSITIONABROAD.com

I just love the name of this website, because it definitely is a transitioning period. There are tons of advice articles from people working abroad and you can only get this type of information from someone going through it. There are also host families who offer great advice to people considering working abroad. The site also links to a lot of work opportunities advertised online. It’s almost like a one stop shop. You can find the perfect job and be prepared for the experience by those who have done it or are still doing it. It is always important to be prepared for what is to come and because this website is more than a job listing site, I find it beneficial.

You are about to start a journey that is going to change your life forever. When applying for work abroad, be sure to find a good CV service, because many countries use a Curriculum Vitae instead of a resume. The process is a bit different, but most of the information is still used. There is something out there for you that is going to be the perfect fit. Don’t give up on this dream, if it is truly what you want to do. You can go on vacation while earning money. Not many people have the guts to go work abroad, but those who have, mostly loved it. I encourage everyone to take some time off and go work in another country. You can always come back if you don’t enjoy it, but you may also decide to stay, if you do.

Have you used other website to help you find a job abroad? Did you like them? Do tell in the comments!
