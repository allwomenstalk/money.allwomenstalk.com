# 9 Transferable Skills That Enhance Your Resume ...

When sending off your resume to a new company, the best plan of action is always to tailor the information in order to make yourself look most qualified and appropriate for the job and role at hand. However, there is always a handful of things that can be included in pretty much every kind of circumstance, things that are seen as being universal in terms of suitability and appropriateness for a new career opportunity. Here are nine **transferable skills that enhance your resume **whatever position you're applying for.

## 1. Critical Thinking

Critical thinking is the talent and ability to make good decisions and take the appropriate actions in any given situations. This is the kind of skill that is important to have listed on your resume because potential employers will be looking for someone who can make good positive choices.

## 2. Collaboration

You need to make them see that you are a good team player because nobody wants to hire someone that can’t get on with their coworkers in a positive and professional manner. You won’t always have to work with others, but the thing is having the ability to be able to do so when you have to.

## 3. Leadership

No matter what kind of position you are applying for, it is always good to display that you have leadership qualities. An important part of being a good worker is the ability to be able to take charge when needed.

## 4. Creativity

Employers like it when they see that you can think outside of the box when you need to; it is one of the best ways to be able to solve a problem in the workplace. Being able to come up with some fresh and new ideas is essential.

## 5. Written Communication

It is really important to possess skills that make you a good communicator, both verbally and in written form. Lots of today’s work communication is via email and other messaging, so it is important that you can put across the kind of professional attitude that bosses want.

## 6. Coding

This is a more technical thing, but computer coding is absolutely the future when it comes to most office work, so it can be helpful to go on a course and gain at least a base level understanding of what it is and how to do it.

## 7. Time Management

Your time at work is time your boss is paying you for, so they want to know that you are someone who can manage that time precisely and professionally.

## 8. Data Management

Another technical thing, but data management is more and more important as the years go by, and you can’t afford to be left behind in your understanding of different programmes, spreadsheets, documents etc.

## 9. Customer Service

You need to be able to display a talent for interacting with customers and clients in a positive manner. Even if your job isn’t necessarily on the front line, you still need to be able to present yourself in that way should you ever need to.
