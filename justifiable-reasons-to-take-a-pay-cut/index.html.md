# 7 Justifiable Reasons to Take a Pay Cut ...

Although most people strive to climb the corporate ladder and increase their salary, there are good reasons to take a pay cut. Given present economic conditions today, most people wouldn't dream of earning less on the job. For them, their income is the only way to maintain a certain standard of living and care for their family. But while we all need adequate income, there are seven justifiable reasons to take a pay cut.

## 1. Your Partner Earns Enough

If you live with a partner who earns enough to compensate for any reduced earnings, this is one of many justifiable reasons to take a pay cut. You might aim to work less if you have young children at home or if you’re battling an illness that makes full-time work challenging. Have an open discussion with your partner to determine whether the family can survive on reduced income.

## 2. You Can Make It on Less

Then again, maybe you're single and providing for yourself. In this case, you need to closely evaluate all your bills and expenditures to see whether you can comfortably afford a pay cut. Earning less might require that you give up needless shopping and convenient services, or that you move into a place with a cheaper rent or mortgage.

## 3. You Have a Sizable Savings

If you're able to survive on less, but you don't have a lot of money in your cash reserves, taking a pay cut might not be the right decision at present. However, if you have at least 3 to 6 months of income in your cash reserves, you’re in a better position to take a job that pays less or reduce your work hours.

## 4. You’ve Exceeded Burnout

If you have a high paying position, you’ve undoubtedly worked hard for this title and pay grade. But unfortunately, the more you give an employer, the less time you have for yourself. Therefore, you may have difficulties achieving a healthy work-life balance. Additionally, your job might affect you physically. If you're fed up and need a break, quitting your current job and taking a position that pays less might keep your sanity intact.

## 5. You Hate Your Job

If you simply hate your job and dread going to work every day, looking for another job can provide the satisfaction you deserve. Unfortunately, another job might pay less than what you're currently earning.

## 6. You Need a Shorter Commute

If you’re not fortunate enough to have a short commute, driving to and from work each day can add one or more hours to your schedule. This can become stressful after a while, especially if you're constantly stuck in traffic or if you have a family. Finding a job closer to home might be the solution, yet this decision might involve less pay.

## 7. You Want to Be Self-employed

Being self-employed is a wonderful feeling, and there's the opportunity to enjoy a flexible schedule. Unfortunately, it takes time to build a business and clientele. If you want to be self-employed, you have to anticipate fluctuations in your income, at least in the beginning.

Taking a pay cut has its set of challenges, but if you thoroughly prepare and count the cost, earning less might make sense.
