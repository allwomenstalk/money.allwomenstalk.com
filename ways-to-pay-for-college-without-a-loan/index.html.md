# 7 Genius Ways to Pay for College without a Loan ...

Looking for some **ways to pay for college without a loan**? Did you know that, on average, the cost of a private, non-profit MBA program including books and housing is $100,000? Law school can cost as much as $150,000 and med school will set you back a cool $200,000. Even more, postgraduate courses at the best universities can set you back between $300,000 and $400,000. With these types of figures flying around, the prospect of college can be daunting, but here are seven effective **ways to pay for college without a loan**.

## 1. Scholarships

This is one of the best **ways to pay for college without a loan**. Do as much research as you can into the different schools that might be able to provide you with scholarship assistance. It can be really worth picking a school that is going to pay your way.

## 2. Financial Aid

Depending on your personal family circumstances, you might be able to qualify for financial aid. This is available to any college student who can demonstrate their position of real financial hardship.

## 3. Diversity Waivers

Allow your diversity to work for you by seeing if you can qualify anywhere to have your tuition fees offset as part of a diversity quota or scheme.

## 4. Free Courses

Sidestep having to pay altogether by taking free courses online instead. Even such prestigious schools as Yale have free courses for people to take at their own leisure over the web!

## 5. Employer Sponsorship

If you commit your future to a specific company, you can often be provided with tuition reimbursement during your time at college. This is seen as a reward for the future service that you will be providing for them.

## 6. Work Study Programs

You can work and study at the same time, earning a stipend while working for the institution that you are going to be studying at.

## 7. Extension Schools

These are less expensive than traditional colleges, but there is no doubting that they give your resume a boost and give you a chance to learn and better yourself at the same time as achieving something that will be worthwhile and respected when it comes to entering the job hunt.
