# 7 Property Flaws You Shouldn't Overlook When Buying a Home

There are several crucial things to take into consideration when buying a home: How much will I spend? Where do I want to live? Is now the right time to buy? As you begin searching for the perfect house, excitement can cloud your judgment, leading to overlooking major property flaws or future expensive repairs such as [foundation issues](#6). To avoid costly regrets, here are seven property flaws you shouldn't overlook when buying a home.

## 1. Older Windows

If you’re buying a home, pay extra attention to the property’s windows and doors. They don't have to be the newest custom styles, but if windows and doors appear to be over 15 or 20 years old, they may not provide adequate thermal insulation. As a result, you might end up paying significantly higher monthly heating and cooling bills. According to [ENERGY STAR](https://www.energystar.gov/) guidelines, upgrading to energy-efficient windows can greatly improve thermal performance and comfort. If replacement is needed, calculate that expense alongside replacing [older appliances](#5) before submitting your offer.

## 2. Musty Odors

Several common factors contribute to persistent home odors, such as pets, trash, and soiled carpeting. These surface-level odors are usually fixable and not necessarily a deal breaker when buying a home. However, if you notice a distinct, persistent musty odor as you walk through the house, there's a strong chance the home has hidden mold growth. The [U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)](https://www.epa.gov/mold) notes that damp environments foster mold spores behind drywall and flooring. If you decide to bid on this house, make sure you schedule a professional mold inspection and make your bid contingent on a satisfactory result.

## 3. Watermarks

Whether these discolored marks are on ceilings or walls, watermarks indicate a past water leak or underlying water damage. This doesn't automatically mean there is an active structural issue with the house today. If the previous homeowner caught the problem early and properly repaired all water leaks, the property is likely mold-free. Still, because water damage often correlates with lingering dampness or [musty odors](#2), it doesn't hurt to have a qualified inspector assess the area before completing the sale.

## 4. Signs of Rodents

Many property buyers open closets to check storage space, but some don’t look underneath cabinets in the kitchen and bathrooms. These dark, hidden areas can quickly reveal what else might live in the house. For example, finding roach bait or mouse droppings indicates you could face a persistent pest problem after move-in. Key red flags to watch for include:

- Unusual droppings under sinks or in cabinets
- Chewed wiring, cardboard boxes, or baseboards
- Unexplained nesting material or strong pest scents

Consulting resources like the [EPA Pest Control Guide](https://www.epa.gov/safepestcontrol) can help identify signs of infestation. Always request a professional pest inspection to ensure the house is completely clear.

## 5. Older Appliances

Most sellers will replace appliances that are completely broken, but they're not obligated to update outdated or aging units. If you inspect a house and notice that the refrigerator, range, dishwasher, or washer and dryer are decades old, consider whether you can afford newer replacements after closing. You can request that the seller update dated models or provide a home warranty credit in your bid, though there are no guarantees they will agree.

## 6. Cracks in the Walls

There are several different reasons for cracks in interior walls. In many instances, hairline cracks simply result from the home settling naturally over the years. However, larger diagonal or wide cracks can signal a serious issue with the home’s structural foundation. If you notice concerning cracks, it’s crucial to arrange for a [professional foundation inspection](https://xpertfoundationrepair.com/top-reasons-to-inspect-foundation-in-san-antonio-a-homeowners-guide/) to assess the situation accurately. Foundation repairs can become exceptionally costly in the future, so evaluate whether you want to take on this potential risk.

## 7. Cut-through Street

Even if the property is located in a quiet subdivision, its specific street might serve as a convenient cut-through to a major roadway or arterial street. Consequently, numerous vehicles may cut through your street during peak morning and evening traffic hours. Ask yourself: am I comfortable managing this level of daily traffic noise and speed? It’s especially critical to consider if you have young children or pets who play outdoors.

| Property Flaw | Primary Risk | Recommended Inspection |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Older Windows | High energy bills & drafts | Window & seal inspection |
| Musty Odors | Hidden mold growth | Professional mold inspection |
| Watermarks | Structural water damage | Roof & plumbing inspection |
