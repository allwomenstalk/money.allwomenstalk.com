# 7 Things You Should Never Lie about on a Resume ...

You might think that lying on a resume to make yourself look better will increase your chances of getting a job. Of course, you'd be wrong. If you lie, then you'll risk getting in trouble. Even if you get away with it all, do you really want a job that you don't deserve? It's better to tell the truth from the start. Here are a few things you should never lie about on a resume:

## 1. Your Skills

![Your Skills](https://img.allw.mn/content/bw/fw/dobs6o3u56a0f2952c371950420862_500x238.gif)

This is the most tempting thing to lie about, but it's also the worst thing to lie about. If you claim that you know how to do something, like use a certain computer program, then your new boss might end up asking you to a job that you have no idea how to do. Do you really want to embarrass yourself by telling everyone that you have no idea how to do something you're supposed to be a pro at already?

## 2. Your Education

![Your Education](https://img.allw.mn/content/cb/qy/fngs191a56a0f295e5097831158940_500x264.gif)

If you don't have a college diploma, don't pretend that you do. There are plenty of jobs that you can get without a college education, and if you try to get a job that requires a college education, you can't just lie about it and expect to get away with it. There aren't any shortcuts in life.

## 3. Your Gaps in Employment

![Your Gaps in Employment](https://img.allw.mn/content/zn/ee/yzovsqjw56a0f29565619975444987_350x197.gif)

Yes, it might look bad if you haven't had a job for the past five years. However, as long as you have a good reason for the employment gap, you can still land a good job. That's why you shouldn't make up fake jobs that you've had in the past. It isn't going to help you.

## 4. Your Criminal Record

![Your Criminal Record](https://img.allw.mn/content/x3/nd/dzzo22iu56a0f2a904ea3023119875_500x281.gif)

If you have a criminal record, you need to mention it. Your boss is going to end up getting a background check on you, which means they'll find out about your past eventually. If you tell them from the get-go, you'll look better than if you lie to them. It pays to be honest.

## 5. Your past Salaries

![Your past Salaries](https://img.allw.mn/content/fh/k3/lv7dmqpc56a0f2956d3e5016810105_500x270.gif)

If your interviewer asks you what you made at your last job, tell the truth. Even if it was a low amount, that doesn't mean that you're going to get offered a low amount at the new company. Besides, your boss will respect your honesty, and don't you want to work for someone who respects you?

## 6. Your GPA

![Your GPA](https://img.allw.mn/content/um/eg/sjbrd39o56a0f2955d02a378622903_350x197.gif)

If you're still in high school or college, then you should resist the urge to lie about your grade point average. It's not all that hard for your employer to get a copy of your school records, which means that your lie is going to get found out. When that happens, you'll be in a sticky situation.

## 7. Your Job Responsibilities

![Your Job Responsibilities](https://img.allw.mn/content/qp/b8/z4crujtu56a0f29734702889061583_340x219.gif)

You're required to list your past jobs and job responsibilities for a reason. It shows your employer how qualified you are. So if you've never done customer service before, don't list it on your resume. It's easy to tell a seasoned worker from a new one. Even if you get the job, someone is going to find out that you got it by fibbing.

If you lie about any of these things, you're only hurting yourself. Have you ever lied on a resume before?
