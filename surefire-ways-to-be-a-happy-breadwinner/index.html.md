# 7 Surefire Ways to Be a Happy Breadwinner ...

Whether you're a wife or husband, or a mother or father, there are ways to be a happy breadwinner. Being the main provider for your family can be stressful and overwhelming at times. And as much as you love your family, you may wish for a different situation. But rather than focus on the hardships, focus on what's going right in your life. Here are seven surefire ways to be a happy breadwinner.

## 1. Be Thankful That You're Able to Provide

Being thankful is one of the best ways to be a happy breadwinner. You may get tired and stressed at times, but you're in a better situation than most people. Some parents and spouses work hard, yet they are unable to adequately provide for their families. Focus on this, and it'll keep a smile on your face.

## 2. Assigned Chores

If you’re working long hours each week, you probably don't have a lot of time to devote to household chores. Rather than overload yourself with extra responsibilities, give each member in your household specific tasks. Getting through the week will require teamwork. And with everyone's cooperation, you'll feel less pressure to stay on top of everything yourself.

## 3. Outsource Housework

Then again, maybe your family also has a busy schedule. This can result in chores getting behind. If there simply isn't enough time in the day to do laundry, cut the grass or complete other basic house work, check your budget to see if you can hire help. Even if it's only for one day a week, this person can take care of tedious assignments around your house, thus helping reduce your stress level

## 4. Be Proud of Your Decision

If you're a female supporting your family, including your husband, be proud of your decision. If you're in a position to take care of your family's financial needs, you may prefer that your husband stay at home with the children. This is a decision that only the two of you can make, and you shouldn’t let others make you feel bad for the choices you've made. If it works for your family -it’s okay.

## 5. Enjoy “me” Time

Find ways to relax and unwind. This is especially important if you have a stressful, demanding job. At least once a week, take time for yourself. Get a massage, a pedicure or go for a long walk by yourself. It also helps to explore a hobby.

## 6. Don’t Let Work Interfere with Family Time

As a breadwinner, you may have several financial obligations on your plate. Not only do you take care of everyday expenses, you might be saving for long-term goals, such as retirement or your kids' education. As a result, you may work long hours to achieve all your goals. This is okay, but not at the expense of family time.

## 7. Learn How to Say “no”

Unfortunately, your job may take over your life. Make sure you find a happy balance. This will not only result in job satisfaction, but reduce guilt from being a working spouse or parent. Resolve only to work a certain amount of hours each week; and if possible, limit the amount of work that you bring home.

You might not be able to change the fact that you're the breadwinner, but you can modify your work habits to ensure that you're happy and content with your decision. What other ways can breadwinners find happiness and satisfaction?
