# 7 Important First Steps to Take when You Lose Your Job ...

This article can offer you some help on the first steps to take when you lose your job. It’s difficult to find yourself in this position. You may find that it’s hard to think about what to do at this point because you feel so overwhelmed. This article can give you some guidance on the first steps to take when you lose your job.

## 1. Part on Good Terms

Number one in the first steps to take when you lose your job is to part on good terms. This isn’t always easy to do. However, it’s wise to follow this advice. You don’t want to burn your bridges. You may need a reference later. It’ll be much easier to get one if you part on good terms now.

## 2. File for Unemployment

If you’re eligible for unemployment, make sure you file for it as soon as you can. It can take a bit to get your first check. If you aren’t sure if you’re eligible then you should look into it. It’s worth trying. Unemployment may not be as much as you were making at your job but it can help fill in the gaps until you find employment again.

## 3. Take a Hard Look at Your Bills

The loss of a job gives you a big change in income. When you’re in this situation it may be time to take a hard look at your bills. What can you cut back on? Are there any bills that can be eliminated? Usually there are a few things that can be tweaked to help you have fewer expenses.

## 4. Update Your Resume

If you’re out of work then you’re more than likely going to be looking for another job. It’s time to break out the old resume. But before you start pounding the pavement, it’s a good to take a look at your resume and see if changes need to be made. It’s something that you definitely want to make sure is updated before you start handing it out. After all, the better the resume, the better your chances of landing a job.

## 5. Clean up Your Social Media

This is something that no one thought about years ago. However, you really need to with modern technology being what it is. Before you start looking for a job, you should take a good look at your social media accounts and see if there’s anything you need to delete. Carefully check your photos too, since they’ll be sure to look at those. There’s a very real chance that a prospective employer could check out your social media activity before they make a decision.

## 6. Take Stock of Your Wardrobe

It’s good to take stock of your wardrobe. See if you have clothing that would be appropriate for an interview. It’s much better to do this now rather than after you get called for an interview. It beats shopping for an outfit in a panic. Knowing you have an interview outfit in your closet can give you a boost of confidence as you job hunt, too.

## 7. Deal with Your Emotions

Lastly, it’s important to deal with your emotions when you lose a job. Losing a job can be very upsetting. You can have many different feelings and that’s okay. What’s important is that you deal with them. You’ll be ready to move on to the next phase of your life much sooner if you do this.

These are 7 things you should do when you lose your job. Have you been through this? You’re welcome to share your experiences.
