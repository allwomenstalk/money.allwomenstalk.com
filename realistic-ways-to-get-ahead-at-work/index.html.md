# 7 Realistic Ways to Get Ahead at Work ...

Knowing the best ways to get ahead at work can take your career to the next level. If you're stuck in a dead-end job, or if you're not happy with your present salary, this could impact how you feel about your job. But rather than sit back and complain, take action to improve your life. Here are seven realistic ways to get ahead at work.

## 1. Avoid Negative Comments

If you seek ways to get ahead at work, do your best to focus on the positive and avoid negative comments. Understandably, there are aspects about your job that you do not like. But if you focus on these, or if you frequently vocalize these feelings, the higher ups may feel that you're not the best person for new positions or promotions.

## 2. Make Good Use of Your down Time

Maybe your position isn't that demanding, and you have down time during the day. Don't just sit at your desk twiddling your thumbs until more work comes your way. Take the initiative. Undoubtedly, there are things that could be done around the office. Maybe a coworker needs help filing paperwork, or perhaps you can assist in other areas. Open your mouth and offer to help. Your willingness will not go unnoticed.

## 3. Accept New Challenges

If an opportunity comes your way, don't turn it down because you question your ability. Your boss or coworker likely sees your potential. This can be your opportunity to shine. If you do a good job on the task, you may be recommended for other important assignments.

## 4. Take a Class or Get an Advanced Degree

Depending on your position, you may only go so far with a high school diploma or a certain degree. If you’ve gone as far as you can with a position, or if you've reached a salary cap, consider returning to school and completing additional courses, or getting an advanced degree. This extra education can provide the skills you need to get ahead at work.

## 5. Work Harder than Everyone else

Your coworkers may spend two or three hours goofing off during the day, but this doesn't mean you have to. Your boss, manager or supervisor is always watching. And when the time comes to promote people, they are going to select those who worked the hardest. Wouldn't it be nice to have your name pop into your supervisor's head?

## 6. Take Advantage of Your Connections

There is nothing wrong with utilizing the resources available to you. If you've established a good relationship with your boss or the owner of your company, let him know your willingness to get ahead and move up with the company. Many companies prefer to promote and hire within the organization. Make others aware of your desire to advance, and you might just go further in your career.

## 7. Carry Yourself in a Professional Manner

At the end of the day, it isn't enough to work hard and make connections. If you don't carry yourself in a professional manner, your supervisor or boss may overlook you for advancement opportunities. Make sure you dress appropriately, use correct English when you speak and avoid profanity.

Moving up the corporate ladder takes time and a lot of hard work. But if you stay determined and take the initiative, you may reach the top quicker. What other tips can you suggest?
