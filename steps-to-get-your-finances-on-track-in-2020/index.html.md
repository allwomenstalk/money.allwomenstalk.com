# 5 Steps to Get Your Finances on Track ...

It's a dream for many people to be completely in control of their finances and be in a comfortable and manageable position. However, to get there, it takes some planning. This planning doesn't have to be difficult or complicated and there are plenty of ways to get your finances on track this year, no matter what kind of situation you may currently find yourself in.

If you are looking to take control of your finances in 2020 then in this article, you will find some useful tips to help you achieve that. One of the most difficult things to take control of is debt, and we will also be looking at how NowLoan can help with that.

## 1. Create a Budget

![Hair, Face, Hairstyle, Eyebrow, Lip,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ub/d0/z7somyk65e90cc3b6cc21695553646_600x729.jpg)

And stick to it! It is so easy to set a budget and over time, steer so far off target that it's impossible to get back to where you were. If you really want a good grasp of your financial situation, it is important to be strict with yourself.

[Setting a budget](https://money.allwomenstalk.com/ways-to-create-a-budget/) doesn't have to mean that you never have anything to spend but it does mean knowing exactly what money is going where. At the start of each year, you should plan out what is going to spend each month. You should mark money for your regular bills such as your rent or mortgage, phone bills, energy bills, etc as well as putting aside some money for a rainy day. You might also factor in annual costs such as any bills you pay yearly and even your summer holiday or money spent on Christmas. This will allow you to know exactly what you need to spend and what you have spare. That being said, don't leave yourself short when it comes to your day to day spending money or else you will run the risk of dipping into your savings or bill money.

## 2. Keep up with the Times

![Eyewear, Sunglasses, Cool, Street fashion, Thigh,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/eb/hz/ailjtzp85e90cc7c6bb59352073015_600x739.jpg)

By this, we mean technology. Gone are the days when you have to be swimming in a sea of paper bills and bank statements. Not only is this super confusing but it can also cause you to miss payments.

There is a wide range of websites and apps available to help you manage your money such as online banking and budgeting apps. Using this in 2020 will eliminate the stress of dealing with paper and will help you to be much more organized and in control.

## 3. Consolidate Your Debts

Does it feel as though you have been paying those credit cards off for years yet the balance doesn't seem to go down? If this sounds familiar, you might want to consider taking out a debt consolidation loan, with which you pay off all your existing debts and then make one (more affordable) monthly payment.

If this is something that you think would benefit your finances, it is important to ensure that you cover all bases and research a variety of loans. This can be time-consuming but using a service such as the one provided by [NowLoan.co.uk](https://www.nowloan.co.uk/payday-loans-alternatives/), you can compare hundreds of loans to find the best one for your personal circumstances.

## 4. Stay on Top of Your Accounts

![Face, Hair, Eyebrow, Skin, Lip,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/kg/kk/awbvpldn5e90cb93cf079773016491_600x739.jpg)

It might sometimes be easier to bury your head in the sand when it comes to your finances but this isn't going to help you in the long run. Making regular checks on all your accounts, (this includes bank accounts, telecoms accounts, credit cards and anything else related to money) can help you stay much more in control of your financial situation and also give you peace of mind that everything is up to date.

## 5. Get Your Credit Rating in Check

![Face, Hair, Eyebrow, Beauty, Skin,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/hx/n7/wgkos6qv5e90cbe442fb9541808134_600x750.jpg)

One of the most detrimental things for anybody looking to improve their finances is having a poor [credit rating](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Credit_rating). It is vital that you keep on top of your credit score and if it isn't looking at its best, get working on improving it.

It might be a daunting task but it isn't as difficult as you might think. Simply by paying your bills on time, getting on the electoral rolls and staying away from your credit limits, you will start to notice a difference and this will then encourage you to keep working on your finances throughout the year.

If you are looking to take better control of your finances in 2020 and set a precedent for the years to come, then these simple tips will help you get started. Whether you are planning to start a strict budget or take advantage of a website such as NowLoan, there are multiple things you can do to stay in control and stress-free when it comes to money.
